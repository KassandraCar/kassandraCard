## Research Focus

My research sits at the intersection of computer science, neuroscience, and systems engineering.  

I am particularly interested in how complex systems — whether neural populations or distributed servers — coordinate, communicate, and maintain stability under constraints.

Across my projects, I explore questions like:

- How do neural systems encode and transmit information?
- How can computational models explain biological behavior?
- How do distributed systems maintain consistency under failure?
- How can low-power embedded systems support biomedical applications?

---

## Neural Modeling & Computational Neuroscience

Through coursework and independent exploration, I have worked with mathematical models such as the Hodgkin–Huxley model to better understand how neurons generate action potentials and transmit signals.

This work involves:

- Modeling differential equations that govern ion channel dynamics  
- Simulating membrane potentials under varying current injections  
- Exploring how parameter changes affect firing behavior  
- Connecting biological interpretation with computational implementation  

This research strengthened my ability to translate biological systems into mathematical abstractions.

---

## Distributed Systems & Replicated State Machines

In distributed systems research and lab work, I implemented protocols such as:

- Primary-backup replication  
- Paxos and Multi-Paxos  
- Sharded key-value stores  
- Fault-tolerant log replication  

These projects required reasoning about:

- Consensus under network partitions  
- Linearizability and safety guarantees  
- Leader election and liveness  
- Garbage collection in replicated logs  

Studying distributed systems deepened my appreciation for fault tolerance — both in machines and in biological systems.

---

## Embedded & Biomedical Systems

My work in embedded systems research includes:

- Designing low-power circuits  
- Programming microcontrollers in C/C++  
- Supporting signal acquisition and backscatter communication  
- Exploring biomedical sensing applications  

In biomedical contexts, constraints matter. Power, signal integrity, and environmental noise all influence system behavior. Designing under these constraints has shaped how I approach engineering problems more broadly.

---

## Research Philosophy

Across neuroscience, distributed systems, and embedded design, I am drawn to one idea:

Complex systems require structure to remain stable.

Whether modeling neurons, coordinating replicated servers, or designing energy-constrained devices, my work focuses on understanding how components interact, fail, recover, and scale.

My long-term goal is to pursue research that bridges computational theory and biological systems — building models and tools that deepen our understanding of intelligence, both artificial and biological.
