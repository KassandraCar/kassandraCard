## Fundraising Coordinator – WashPIRG UW

As Fundraising Coordinator for WashPIRG at the University of Washington, I led student-driven fundraising initiatives to support advocacy campaigns focused on environmental sustainability, public health, and civic engagement.

WashPIRG operates at the intersection of policy, grassroots organizing, and student activism. My role was to ensure that our campaigns had the financial infrastructure necessary to operate effectively.

---

## What the Role Involved

Fundraising at WashPIRG required both strategic planning and direct engagement. My responsibilities included:

- Organizing and managing campus fundraising drives  
- Coordinating student volunteers for outreach efforts  
- Communicating campaign impact to donors  
- Tracking funding goals and progress  
- Ensuring transparency in financial reporting  

This meant translating complex policy goals into clear, compelling narratives that resonated with students.

---

## Skills Developed

The role strengthened my ability to:

- Communicate persuasively and ethically  
- Set measurable goals and track performance  
- Manage teams under time constraints  
- Handle rejection and remain resilient  
- Align mission-driven work with operational structure  

Fundraising is often invisible labor, but it is foundational. Without sustainable funding, even the most well-intentioned campaigns cannot move forward.

---

## Why It Mattered

WashPIRG campaigns addressed issues such as:

- Environmental protection  
- Affordable healthcare  
- Consumer rights  
- Civic participation  

Supporting these efforts required not only passion, but structure.

Serving as Fundraising Coordinator taught me that impact is not just about ideas — it is about systems that sustain those ideas.

---

## Reflection

This experience reshaped how I think about infrastructure. Whether in advocacy, nonprofits, or technology, long-term impact depends on sustainable systems.

Fundraising is a form of systems design. It requires understanding people, incentives, communication, and resource allocation — all skills that translate directly into engineering leadership.

At WashPIRG, I learned how to build momentum, sustain engagement, and connect mission with measurable action.
